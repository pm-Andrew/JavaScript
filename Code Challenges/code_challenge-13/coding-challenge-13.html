Different Types of Note-Taking Methods

1. Outline Method

  • Structure: Hierarchical, using bullet points or numbers.
  • Best for: Lectures or reading with clear organization.

Ex.

                    I. Main Topic
                    A. Subtopic
                        1. Detail
                        2. Another Detail

2. Cornell Method

Structure: Divides the page into 3 sections: Cue column, Note-taking column, and Summary section.

Best for: Active recall and exam prep.

Example:
Cues / Questions Notes
What is photosynthesis?
Why is sunlight important? 		Photosynthesis is the process by which green plants use sunlight to synthesize food from carbon dioxide and water.
Sunlight provides energy for the reaction.
Occurs in chloroplasts.
Summary:
Photosynthesis converts sunlight, water, and CO₂ into energy (glucose), taking place in plant cell chloroplasts.

3. Sentence Method

Structure: Each new thought or fact gets its own line or sentence.

Best for: Fast-paced lectures when structure is unclear.

Example:

                    1. The mitochondria is the powerhouse of the cell.
                    2. It converts glucose into ATP through cellular respiration.
                    3. ATP is the main energy currency of the cell.
                    4. This process requires oxygen and releases carbon dioxide.

4. Boxing Method

Structure: Ideas grouped into boxes on the page.

Best for: Tablets or digital notes, organizing distinct sections clearly.

Example:

  • --- Box 1 ---
  • Definition: A function is a reusable block of code that performs a specific task.
  • --- Box 2 ---
  • Syntax: def function_name(parameters):     # function body

5. Charting Method

Structure: Notes organized into a table with columns.

Best for: Comparing topics, especially for classes like history or science.

Example:

Topic Definition Example
Concept 1 Descrip. Ex.
Concept 2 Descrip. Ex.
